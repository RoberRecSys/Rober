Natural Language Explainable Recommendation
with Robustness Enhancement

1. Abstract

Natural language explainable recommendation aims to provide users with textual explanations for recommendations. Previous studies has demonstrated that recommendation models are vulnerable to performance degradation when exposed to small yet targeted perturbations. While these studies primarily focus on the robustness of classification tasks associated with specific category labels, similar challenges arise in generating explanations, which is essentially sequence generation task. Notably, we observe that for similar user-item pairs, the generated explanations may differ significantly. This inconsistency in explanations can potentially lead to user confusion and undermine the credibility of the system. To address this concern, we formulate the robustness objective for generating recommendation explanations and propose a robustness-enhanced framework based on adversarial learning. Specifically, when acquiring targeted adversarial examples, we consider two crucial characteristics of textual explanations: the variance in word importance and the flexibility in word semantics. To capture these characteristics, we design dedicated methods for importance estimation and synonyms mining for each word in the sentence. Moreover, to establish effective collaboration between these characteristics, we develop a joint learning framework based on multi-agent reinforcement learning, where each task is assigned an agent that is coordinated through a shared reward mechanism. We conduct extensive experiments based on three real-world datasets to verify the performance and robustness improvements of our framework.

Contributions

  • We formulate the robustness objective in sequence generation and propose a robustness-enhanced framework for natural language explainable recommendation.To the best of our knowledge, it it the first time in the recommendation domain.
  • To model the two key characteristics of textual explanations, we design both context-free and context-aware methods for importance estimation and synonyms mining of the words in the sequence. Furthermore, a multi-agent reinforcement learning based framework is developed to achieve effective collaboration between these tasks.
  • Extensive experiments on three real-word datasets demonstrate the improvements achieved by our framework on the quality and robustness of explanations.
